02  The scorecard

What Ooma already gets right, and the fruit hanging lowest

The foundation is better than most. A few fixable issues are quietly capping how often AI engines can find and cite the brand.

What is working

Keyword strategy and site architecture

Ranks on strong head terms (business phone system, business VoIP phone, cloud phone systems) on a clean, logical site structure. The demand and the scaffolding are already there.

Use of structured data

Breadcrumb schema on the homepage, plus article, breadcrumb, and video schema across the blog. The habit of marking up content is in place to build on.

Clean CTAs and forms

Clear conversion paths and uncluttered forms. Once more traffic arrives from AI surfaces, the page is ready to convert it.

Positive brand sentiment and forum presence

Sentiment reads positive across the web: customers consistently praise service quality, cost-effective plans, and support. That is exactly the raw signal LLMs synthesize when they decide who to recommend.

Low-hanging fruit

03  The critical fix

The indexation issue, in one panel

This is the one that matters most, and the one I would unblock before touching anything else.

Root cause

AI crawlers hit a wall before they ever see the site

Ooma.com sits behind Incapsula bot management that serves automated crawlers an error page (an Incapsula incident ID) instead of the homepage. robots.txt actually permits the AI bots (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and Google-Extended all scored as allowed), so the block is happening one layer up, at the edge. The policy says yes while the infrastructure says no, and the content LLMs need to read and cite may never be reachable.

The fix, and why it cascades

Allowlist the known AI and search crawlers at the Incapsula edge by verified user agent and reverse-DNS, so GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, and Googlebot get a 200 and server-rendered content instead of an incident ID. The Quotable, Understandable, and Trustworthy pillars score near zero only because the tools are grading an error page. The real site has headings, copy, and schema. Open this one door and three of the four pillars have room to move at once, with no new content required.

04  Where I would take it

Next-level plays, once the basics are clean

This is where a GEO program stops playing defense and starts compounding. Three plays I would build for Ooma.

Automated internal linking

A programmatic engine that maps topic and entity clusters and auto-links related pages, so crawlers get a clean, navigable graph and authority flows to the pages that should win each query. Scales without a content team babysitting it.

Crawl depth and authority

Brand sentiment scoring and alignment

Ooma's sentiment is already positive. I would score it continuously across Reddit, forums, review sites, and UGC, then align messaging so every surface tells one consistent story. Models synthesize sentiment from everywhere at once, so consistency becomes a ranking input.

Cross-channel positioning

Live answer-engine share tracking

A weekly monitor of Ooma's share of answer across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and AI Overviews that flags every new competitor citation, paired with an original-data flywheel (a real SMB calling-cost study) that gives engines something only Ooma can be cited for.

A few things I believe about AI search

LLMs qualify brands before humans click

That shift drives a surge in branded search and raises the bar on machine readability. Which is exactly why Ooma's crawler block is so costly.

AI cites original work, not restated consensus

Proprietary data and well-structured comparison content get pulled into answers. Echoing the category gives the engine no reason to name you.

Positioning has to align across every surface

Owned content, social, forums, reviews, and UGC are all inputs now. A consistent position is a ranking factor, not just a brand exercise.
